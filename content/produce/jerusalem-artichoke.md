Not from Jerusalem, not an artichoke — a sunflower's root, and the name is a mishearing of the Italian *girasole*. It looks like ginger's knobbly cousin, and most people leave it at the market for exactly that reason, unsure how to peel something so irregular. The trick is not to bother: scrub the skin, roast it whole, and it turns soft and faintly nutty, somewhere between an artichoke heart and a good potato. Best eaten with people you know well — it has a reputation, gently, for making itself felt afterward.
